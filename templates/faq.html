FAQ'S

What is the NYT Bestseller List?

The New York Times Bestseller list is one of the most prominent lists of bestselling books in the United States. It is an important marker for publishing success but because of it's popularity, has become a great way to bolster sales and determine a book’s popularity. The Bestseller list is not just one list, but rather several lists grouped by genre and format, some published weekly and others on a monthly basis.

What are the criteria to get on the list?

Exactly how the Times compiles the list is a "trade secret", however the list uses sales figures, other data and internal guidelines that are unpublished. The manner in which they analyze sales data is based on a proprietary method, however, "requires an author to make a minimum of 5000 book sales (higher, depending on the list) in a single week across diverse retailers and from multiple geographic locations."

How long has the list existed?

The Bestseller list was created in October 12, 1931 and since then The New York Times Book Review has published the list weekly. The list has expanded through the decades and has grown to include many more genres and categories.

What are Imprints?

In publishing, an imprint is a trade or brand name under which a publisher publishes their work, this is typically done to target specific consumer market groups. Essentially, imprints are subsidiaries of larger publishing comapnies, and the name of the imprint is what appears on the book as the publisher.

What are the Big 5?

The "Big 5" sometimes referred to as the "Top 5" the Big 5 consists of the five biggest publishing companies in the USA. These are: Hachette Book Group, HarperCollins Publishers, Macmillan Publishers, Penguin Random House, and Simon & Schuster.
