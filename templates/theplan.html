THE PLAN PAGE

KNOW YOUR GOALS

The NYT Bestseller list is notoriously both easy and hard to get on. While the requirements seem straightforward (a minimum of 5k units sold within one week), that is by far the only metric. Ultimately, it depends on the editor’s decisions in The Best-Seller Lists Desk of The New York Times News Department, so any aspiring bestseller needs to come to their attention! While most business can be conducted digitally, also consider visiting the big publishing houses.

BUILD HYPE

Marketing is all about getting your product in front of the right eyes. Historically with books, that would be mostly achieved through bookstores, bookstore chains, and libraries. Now the key is to market across multiple digital and physical platforms; social media is a wonderful way to do that! After all, even if your book makes it to the recommended table in Barnes and Nobles, there is still a shareable photo opportunity! BookTok has taken the world by storm, but it is far from the only platform. Joining many groups on each platform will allow you to tune into the consumer base’s preferences.

PRESALES

An excellent way to boost those first week sales is building hype! Consider something special like edge art or a special limited edition cover art that will entice buyers to pre-order! That way you will have guaranteed sales for a full week. Finding a good marketing team will also help you on your way!

DON'T GIVE UP

Once you are on the list, it seems easier to stay on the list for a number of reasons; in no small part because of the additional advertising being on the list at all provides. You will find it easier to get author signings at local or chain bookstores, and every website looks better with the words NYT BESTSELLER!
